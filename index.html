Our Mission

Why we built NEXIS

Human language is beautiful — but it's also full of habits, filler, and inefficiencies we never questioned. NEXIS doesn't replace your language. It sharpens it. Think of it as a tool that makes communication cleaner, faster, and more universal.

🧠

Inspired by AI

When AI systems communicate with each other, they naturally strip language down to pure meaning. NEXIS applies that insight — but keeps it fully human-friendly.

⚡

Up to 60% Faster

By eliminating articles, redundant verb forms, and filler words, NEXIS sentences are dramatically shorter without losing any meaning.

🌍

Universal by Design

No irregular verbs. No gendered nouns. No confusing spelling rules. NEXIS is built to be equally easy for speakers of any native language.

🧒

For All Ages

A 7-year-old can learn the basics in one afternoon. Adults will master it in days. The simplicity is the point — and the power.

🔧

Enhances, Not Replaces

NEXIS isn't here to replace English or any other language. It's an additional tool — like shorthand was for writers, or notation is for musicians.

🚀

Alive & Growing

NEXIS is open. As more people use it, it grows. New compound words emerge. New speakers shape its future. It belongs to everyone.

🙋 Lesson 2: Pronouns — Who is talking?

NEXIS has just 4 pronouns. That's it. No "he/she/it/him/her/his/hers" — just one word for each concept. This alone saves enormous time.

First person

mi = I / me / my

Used for yourself in any position. "mi go" = I go. "fo mi" = for me. "mi bon" = I am good.

Second person

yu = you / your

"yu hap?" = are you happy? "fo yu" = for you. "yu bon" = you are good.

Third person

da = they / them / their

Gender-neutral by default. "da go" = they go. "fo da" = for them. One word, no confusion.

First plural

wi = we / us / our

"wi et" = we eat. "fo wi" = for us. "wi bon" = we are good.

mi hap-tim yuI greet you (hello to you)
wi + lov daWe really love them
yu bon?Are you good / how are you?
da no se miThey didn't say (anything to) me

⏰ Lesson 3: Tense & Time — Past, Present, Future

In English you change the verb: "go / went / will go." In NEXIS, the verb never changes. Instead, place one tiny marker before it.

–

Past

mi – go
I went

∅

Present (no marker)

mi go
I go / I am going

+

Future

mi + go
I will go

mi – et pre-timI ate earlier / yesterday
da + ke nauThey will arrive soon
wi – don bon wokWe did good work
yu + sle pos?Will you sleep later?

⚡ Lesson 4: Intensity — Say More With Less

Instead of "very", "really", "extremely", "a little bit" — NEXIS uses one-symbol modifiers placed before any word. This is one of NEXIS's most powerful features.

Extreme

++ = extremely

"mi ++ hap" = I am extremely happy. "++ fas!" = incredibly fast / emergency!

High

+ = very / really

"yu + bon" = you are really good. "mi + wan et" = I really want to eat.

Slight

~ = a little / somewhat

"mi ~ sle" = I'm a little tired. "da ~ bad" = they're somewhat wrong.

Question

? = question (end)

Place at the end: "yu + hap?" = are you very happy? Any sentence becomes a question with ?

mi ++ lov yuI love you extremely much
da ~ bonThey are somewhat good / okay-ish
wi + nid ayd nauWe really need help right now
yu ~ sle?Are you a little tired?

🗣️ Lesson 5: Full Sentences — Putting It Together

NEXIS word order is always: Subject → Verb → Object. Never changes. This removes almost all ambiguity. Add tense markers and intensity and you can express nearly anything.

Rule

Subject first

Who is doing the action. Always first: "mi", "yu", "da", "wi" or a noun.

Rule

Verb second

What is happening. Add tense marker before if needed: "go", "+ go", "– go".

Rule

Object third

Who or what the action affects. "mi go lo" = I go (to) place. "mi et bon" = I eat good (food).

Compounds

Join roots with –

"hap-tim" = hello. "pos-tim" = later. "he-bad" = sick. Build any concept from the core words.

mi + wan et. yu hap et?I really want to eat. Are you happy to eat? (hungry?)
wi – don + bon wok pre-timWe did very good work earlier / yesterday
yu + ayd mi lern hau don wo?Can you help me learn how to do this?
da – no ke. mi ~ bad fo da.They didn't come. I feel a little bad for them.
mi ++ lov wi lo.I love our place extremely much.
